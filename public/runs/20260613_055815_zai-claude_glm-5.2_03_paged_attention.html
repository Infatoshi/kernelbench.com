What's in the kernel

  • _decode_kernel: one program per (kv_split, batch, kv_head). Loads the GQA group's queries once, streams its slice of the paged KV cache (K/V read from the packed [K|V] pointer at offsets 0/HEAD_DIM — no materialize), computes QKᵀ/softmax@V with online flash softmax, writes per-split partials (m, l, acc) in fp32.
  • _reduce_kernel: vectorized flash-merge over splits → bf16 output.
  • CUDA graph capture: the two-kernel sequence is captured on the first stable call and replayed via an identity-checked hot path — this was the single biggest small-shape win (cuts ~6µs of Triton launch overhead to ~2µs of cuGraphLaunch).

Key findings (profiled with ncu)

  • Decode is latency-bound, not compute-bound (~36–49% of cycles stalled on HBM/L1TEX scoreboard deps). The fix was a deep smem pipeline (num_stages=5–6) over a small BLOCK_N=32, which fills the prefetch queue without overflowing shared memory. More warps (8) help only the shortest sequence; 16 hurts (register pressure).
  • Two kernels beat an in-kernel fused reduce here: with the graph the reduce launch is free, and the pure decode kernel schedules better than one carrying fence/atomic/reducer code.
  • The big shape saturates HBM at 0.83; the small shapes are bounded by total-work-limited parallelism + the ~2µs graph-replay floor (the main residual gap between kernel-only ~0.74 and official ~0.68).
