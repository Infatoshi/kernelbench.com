Summary

solution.py is a real fused unpack+dequant+GEMM kernel (no banned ops), passes python check.py (all 5 shapes × 3 seeds × numeric-stress cases), and benchmarks at geomean peak_fraction ≈ 0.32 (2.3× over the reference-style baseline of 0.14).

Per-shape (DRAM-bandwidth fraction, regime=memory):

| shape | M | N | K | path | frac | |---|---|---|---|---|---| | 0 | 1 | 12288 | 4096 | CUDA GEMV | 0.55 (994 GB/s) | | 1 | 32 | 12288 | 4096 | Triton GEMM | 0.36 | | 2 | 256 | 12288 | 4096 | Triton GEMM | 0.13 (168 TFLOPS, near 0.15 compute ceiling) | | 3 | 1 | 4096 | 4096 | CUDA GEMV | 0.30 | | 4 | 16 | 14336 | 4096 | Triton GEMM | 0.45 |

Two kernel paths:

  • Decode (M=1): CUDA C++ GEMV via load_inline on a one-time-transposed weight (N, K//2), one warp per output, 32 lanes doing K/32 of the dot product + warp shuffle-reduce, vectorized 16-byte loads with next-iteration prefetch (1-deep for large N, 2-deep for small N to add memory-level parallelism). This broke the ~750 GB/s ceiling that capped a Triton tl.sum-based GEMV (ncu showed the Triton path was stall-bound at 32% occupancy).
  • Prefill (M≥16): Triton tl.dot GEMM with fused per-group dequant, even/odd-K nibble split so each packed byte is read once. num_warps=8 roughly doubled M=16/32 bandwidth.

Buffers (transposed weight, output, partials) are cached on the module and returned persistently to cut per-call launch overhead.

What I couldn't crack (the remaining gap): the M=16/32 Triton GEMM is register-bound (166 regs/thread → 16% occupancy). I tried a hand-written bf16 wmma tensor-core GEMM but hit torch's -D__CUDA_NO_BFLOAT16_CONVERSIONS__ + multi-arch sm_75 "incomplete type" issue, then scrambled output I couldn't quickly debug — so I kept the Triton GEMM rather than risk the working solution. CUTLASS mixed-input GEMM or a more careful wmma implementation is the path to lift those two shapes further.

One process-hygiene lesson worth flagging: I accidentally wedged the shell by running several load_inline CUDA compiles concurrently — saved to memory so that doesn't recur.
