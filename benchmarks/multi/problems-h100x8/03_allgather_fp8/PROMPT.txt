I need you to write a fast fp8 all-gather with on-the-fly dequant for an 8xH100 SXM node (NVLink4, ~900 GB/s/GPU, NVSwitch). Runs across all 8 GPUs under torchrun. Reference is in reference.py, your kernel goes in solution.py — same Model, get_inputs, get_init_inputs interface, must work at world_size=8. Make a mess if you want; final answer in solution.py.

The op: each rank holds a bf16 shard of shape (tokens, hidden). You must fp8-quantize it (amax-scaled e4m3), all-gather the fp8 shards across all 8 ranks, and produce the full (world*tokens, hidden) tensor dequantized back to bf16 (each source rank's shard times its own scale). This is the FSDP/TP weight-gather pattern. Crucially the bytes on the wire are fp8 — 1 byte per element, half a bf16 gather — so quantize, move the fp8 bytes, and dequant locally; do not gather in bf16. Read reference.py for the exact semantics (it gathers a uint8 byte-view, which is the fp8 storage, and quantizes internally so the scale adapts to magnitude).

Graded purely on busbw — fraction of NVLink peak, geomean over shapes. Forbidden: every torch.distributed collective (all_gather, all_reduce, reduce_scatter, all_to_all, the c10d/functional variants). Implement the gather yourself over NVLink with CUDA symmetric memory (peers read each other's shard buffers directly), NVSHMEM, or a hand-rolled ring of p2p send/recv. Keep the payload fp8 across the wire and fuse the dequant into the write-out. Look up symmetric-memory / NVSHMEM docs and headers yourself.

Correctness tolerance on the bf16 output is 5e-2 abs/rel (fp8 is noise-dominated), across every shape, 5 rank-distinct trials, and numeric-stress rescales. Shapes (tokens is the per-rank shard): tokens=2048 hidden=8192 (large), tokens=256 hidden=8192 (transitional), tokens=32 hidden=8192 (small, latency-bound), tokens=2048 hidden=8191 (off-alignment hidden).

Flywheel: implement, profile (nsys/ncu — confirm the wire payload is fp8 and the dequant overlaps), time with benchmark.py, verify with `python check.py`. If check.py hasn't printed PASS, you're not done. Take as long as you need to actually push the busbw up.
